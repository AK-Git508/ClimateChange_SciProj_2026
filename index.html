A Global Crisis... Caused by Humans

Evidence #1: Global Temperature Spike

Modern fever: a rapid climb that began in 1970.

Observation: Global surface temperatures remained mostly stable until the late 1800s. Since 1970, there has been a rapid and steady climb in temperature. The total increase is now more than 1.0°C above the historical baseline.

Reasoning: This rapid temperature increase is a major and very urgent “red flag” for our planet. This is because the Earth’s climate system is massive and usually changes slowly. Natural forces such as volcanic eruptions or shifts in Earth’s orbit cannot explain a 1.1°C jump in just a few decades. The timing of this jump aligns with rapid global industrialization and increased fossil fuel use.

Evidence #2: Greenhouse Gas Concentrations

The chemical culprits: CO₂, methane, and nitrous oxide rising together.

Observation: CO₂, methane, and nitrous oxide were extremely stable for nearly 1,900 years. All three gases show a simultaneous spike starting in the late 1800s.

Reasoning: This indicates the atmospheric change is linked to human activity. These gases trap heat through the greenhouse effect. As industrialization expanded and fossil fuels were burned, these gases increased, which helps explain why global temperatures are now rising.

Evidence #3: Breaking the Natural Cycle

800,000 years of stability ends in a sharp rise.

Observation: For millennia, CO₂ naturally cycled around an average of 300 ppm. The current level has surged to around 420 ppm, breaking an 800,000-year pattern.

Reasoning: This shows that the current warming is not part of a normal natural cycle. Human activity has pushed atmospheric carbon dioxide levels beyond anything seen in nearly a million years.

Summary - Connecting the Evidence

When scientists examine all three pieces of evidence together, a clear story begins to form. The temperature graph shows that the Earth remained relatively stable for centuries before experiencing a rapid warming starting in the late 20th century. This sudden shift indicates that something significant disrupted the natural climate balance. The second piece of evidence helps explain the cause of this warming. Greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide began rising sharply during the same time period that industrial activity expanded around the world. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere, meaning that as their concentration increases, global temperatures naturally rise as well. Finally, the long-term carbon dioxide record shows that today’s atmospheric levels are far outside the natural range observed over the past 800,000 years. Natural climate cycles never pushed carbon dioxide levels anywhere near the levels measured today.ß When these three findings are combined, they create a logical chain of cause and effect. Human industrial activity increased greenhouse gas emissions, those gases trap heat in the atmosphere, and that trapped heat is driving the rapid rise in global temperatures seen today.

What Happens Next?

The evidence is clear: Human activity has pushed us past natural limits. Now that we know the cause, we have the power to change the trend.

Call to Action

What can we humans do to help reduce this crisis?

  • -- Reduce energy consumption by using public transport, biking, or walking.
  • -- Switch to renewable energy sources like solar and wind power.
  • -- Plant trees and support reforestation efforts.
  • -- Reduce waste and recycle more.
  • -- Advocate for climate-friendly policies and support sustainable businesses.
